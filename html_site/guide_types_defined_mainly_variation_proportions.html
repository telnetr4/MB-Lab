Complex types defined by the variation of proportions, mass and muscles

This category of types is the most varying scene. It includes some very specific characters, defined by the most diffused idealistic proportions for male and female, the exaggerate proportions for comic characters and finally the average proportions of common people.

A good example of this category for female characters are

  • Hero01
  • Hero02
  • Ideal shapely
  • Ideal fashion industry

These types are defined by the idealistic proportions based on the modern canons.

The "Hero01" and "Hero02" are types often used in action comics: shapely bodies, with hyper-long legs, slim and athletic physique. "Hero01" is a strong character, while "Hero02" is more feminine and agile.

The other two types are for more realistic characters, defined by two different "ideal" canons.

The "shapely" one is more soft, with noticeable curves and an average percentage of muscles, while the "fashion" one is based on the proportions used by the fashion industry.

Some typical complex types for male are

  • Hero01
  • Hero02
  • Idealistic proportions
  • Brute
  • Fat brute
  • Evil genius

The types for man proportions are different from the woman set.

There are some more variances in comic-exaggerated types and only one idealistic type.

The "Hero01" is developed using the proportions for common action comics: the "strong man" that can play the role of hero or antihero.

The "Hero02" is for secondary or alternative heroes, it is the "stocky man" closer to a body-builder, except for the long arms and the strong bones, huge feet and hands to emphasize its strength.

The "Brute" type is based on a common stereotype for the brute force character, basically a mutation of Hero02.

The "Fat Brute" is a common character usually adopted for evil enemies. A mix of muscle power and heavy mass, huge shoulders, strong bones and little head.

The "Evil genius" type is the opposite of the "Fat Brute", to emphasize the cerebral power of his brain.
